CORN PUDDING

I was searching for a side dish for our upcoming Easter family dinner until I stumbled upon this corn pudding dish on social media. This dish caught my attention because corn is one of my favorite ingredients. I also found it interesting that it uses two types of corn: whole kernels and creamed style. There are different kinds of corn puddings out there, but this specific recipe is a simple one consisting of easy to find ingredients. Corn PuddingIn this recipe, the whole kernels and creamed style corn are baked in a mixture of milk and eggs. The result is the perfect side dish to accompany a special dinner like Easter, Christmas or Thanksgiving. The taste and texture is just perfect and the dish is not too sweet. The best part, as a corn lover, is that I can taste the corn in every bite. Although this is a side dish, I also like to have it as a snack. Enjoy!

DETAILED INGREDIENTS:

  • 4 eggs, at room temperature
  • 1/3 cup butter, melted
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1/2 cups milk
  • 4 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 can (341 ml) whole kernel corn, drained
  • 1 can (398 ml) can cream-style corn

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Grease an 8-inch square glass baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, lightly beat eggs. Add melted butter, sugar, vanilla, and salt; beat well. Set aside.
  3. Pour milk into a liquid measuring cup. Add cornstarch and whisk until cornstarch is dissolved. Whisk in cornstarch/milk mixture into the egg mixture. Stir in corn and creamed corn; mix well.
  4. Pour mixture into prepared baking dish and bake for 1 hour.
